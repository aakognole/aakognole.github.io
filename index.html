Abhishek A. Kognole

Postdoctoral Research Fellow, University of Maryland Baltimore

Hi there! Welcome to my webpage.

I am a researcher with interdisciplinary aptitude having chemical engineering background followed by strong research accomplishment in computational chemistry and biophysics. My research interests involve applying the super-computing facilities to understand the relationship between structure and function of biomolecules like proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates and small drug-like molecules.

In my earlier career as graduate research assistant I applied computational approach on wide range of projects, from characterization of enzymes involved in biomass degradation for biofuel production to identifying function of proteins secreted in mammalian cancer cells. Recently, I have worked on understanding how metal ions facilitate the negatively charged RNA molecules to achieve complex tertiary structures that are necessary for their functions. Additionally, I am a part of team that is specialized in development of polarizable force-field for molecular dynamics simulation. Currently, I am leading a project to transfer a structure-based drug design platform to target RNA molecules with small molecules. In future, I am interested in using the computational approach towards understanding the functions of biomolecules and their interactions with drug-like small molecules, eventually applying it for development of clinical candidates.
